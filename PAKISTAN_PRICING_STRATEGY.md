# MyCyber DLP — Pricing Strategy for Pakistani Firms

## Target Segments

| Segment | Size | Pain Point | Entry Price |
|---------|------|-----------|-------------|
| Freelancers / Solo consultants | 1 person | Client asks "how do you protect my data?" | PKR 0 (Free) |
| Small agencies / law firms / clinics | 5–20 staff | Leaked CNICs in spreadsheets, email attachments | PKR 4,500/mo (Pro) |
| Fintech / e-commerce / SaaS startups | 20–100 staff | Buyer due-diligence, compliance questionnaires | PKR 15,000/mo (Enterprise) |
| Banks / telcos / large enterprises | 500+ staff | PDPA audit, multi-tenant, API integration | Custom (PKR 50,000+/mo) |

## How to Pitch Each Segment

### Freelancers / Micro-businesses
- Use the **Free plan** as a lead magnet.
- One viral use-case: paste a client spreadsheet into the scanner, screenshot the CNIC hits, share on LinkedIn/X.
- Upgrade trigger: "You hit 100 scans/month — upgrade to Pro for PKR 4,500 to keep scanning."

### Small Professional Firms (law, CA, clinics)
- **Pain**: Patient/client CNICs leaked in email attachments = PDPA liability + reputational risk.
- **Pitch**: "For the price of one dinner with a client, you can prove you have data-leak controls."
- **Close tactic**: Annual plan with 2 months free = PKR 45,000/year. Frame as "less than PKR 125 per day."

### Fintech / E-commerce / SaaS Startups
- **Pain**: Enterprise buyers demand SOC-2 / security questionnaire answers. No DLP = deal delayed or lost.
- **Pitch**: "MyCyber gives you a live security dashboard you can screenshare in a buyer call."
- **Close tactic**: Offer a **pilot program** — "Pay after 30 days only if your compliance officer approves the report."
- Enterprise plan (PKR 15,000) includes API access so they can embed scanning into their own product pipeline.

### Banks / Telcos / Large Enterprises
- **Pain**: PDPA compliance audits, multi-department data governance, zero-trust requirements.
- **Pitch**: "Custom deployment with multi-tenancy, on-premise option, and PDPA-readiness documentation."
- **Close tactic**: Do **not** quote a price on the first call. Ask for an RFP / pilot scope. Start at PKR 50,000/month and negotiate based on user seats and scan volume.

## Tactical Pricing Rules

1. **Always anchor against foreign cost** — "US tools cost PKR 1.1M/year. We cost PKR 45,000/year."
2. **Annual pre-pay is king** — offer 2 months free on annual to improve cash flow.
3. **Pilot pricing** — first enterprise customer gets 50% off for 3 months in exchange for a public case study.
4. **Never discount the monthly** — if they want cheaper, push them to annual.
5. **Add seats, not plans** — Enterprise should be priced per-seat after 10 users (e.g., PKR 1,500/user/month beyond 10).

## Revenue Math (Conservative)

Assume you close:
- 50 Pro customers at PKR 45,000/year (annual)
- 5 Enterprise at PKR 150,000/year
- 1 Custom at PKR 600,000/year

**ARR = PKR 3,525,000 (~$12,600 USD)**

At this stage you are investable. Angels and pre-seed funds in Pakistan (e.g., Sarmayacar, i2i) look for:
- PKR 2M+ ARR
- 20%+ MoM growth
- 1 anchor enterprise customer

## Bottom Line for Founders

> **Do not overthink the price.** Pakistani firms buy on trust, social proof, and ease of payment (JazzCash / Easypaisa / bank transfer). Start at PKR 4,500/mo for Pro, get 10 paying customers, then raise the price. Your first 10 customers matter more than your pricing spreadsheet.
